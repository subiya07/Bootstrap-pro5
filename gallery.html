Hidden Gems You Must Visit While Exploring Greece

The charismatic island at the edge of the Dodecanese lies less than 2,625 feet from the Turkish coast and fiercely guards its Greek identity. For millennia the tiny island has acted as a crossroads for seafaring communities and major civilizations. The birthplace of Zeus offers panoramic views, Byzantine monasteries, and stunning contrasts between the Mediterranean's blue waters and white sands. By exploring Kastellorizo, you can immerse yourself in history and antiquity that few other places can rival. From the Neolithic era to the knights of St John and 19th-century fishing heritage, the remote and picture-perfect village highlights Greece's past..

The stunning island of Ithaca gained prominence thousands of years ago with a mention in the Homeric legend The Odyssey. The Ionian island retains its mythical allure with isolated sandy beaches and picturesque fishing villages. Boutique hotels offer a touch of contemporary luxury and the fjord-like bay shimmers alongsidethe iconic architecture. With the pairing of lively cafes and more than 30 archeological sites,this island provides a unique experience.
